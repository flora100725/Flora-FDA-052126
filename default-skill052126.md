# TFDA Medical Device Premarket Review Guidelines (Master Skills Profile)

This document defines the automated verification logic, evaluation rules, and legal standards required under the **Regulations Governing Issuance of Medical Device Licenses, Registration, and Annual Reporting (醫療器材許可證核發與登錄及年度申報準則)**.

---

## 1. Administrative Requirements (Table 1 & Table 2)

### 1.1 Medical Device Merchant License (醫療器材商許可執照影本)
*   **Manufacturing Applicants (製造):** Must submit a valid copy of their Medical Device Manufacturing License (醫療器材製造業許可執照影本).
*   **Importing Applicants (輸入):** Must submit a copy of their Medical Device Retail/Wholesale License listing "Import" (輸入) in its approved business scope.
*   **Domestic Contract Manufacturing (國內委託製造):** Must submit the business licenses of both the contractor (委託者) and the contract manufacturer (受託製造業者).

### 1.2 Quality Management System Compliance (QMS/GMP)
*   **Standard Requirement:** Submit a copy of the QMS/GMP compliance certificate (醫療器材品質管理系統準則之證明文件影本) issued by the central competent authority.
*   **Reclassified Drug Transition:** If the item was previously regulated as a drug and reclassified as a medical device, check if a valid Drug GMP certificate is submitted within the 3-year transition allowance (Table 2 Note 6(2)).

### 1.3 China-Origin Import Restrictions
*   For imported medical devices manufactured in China, verify the presence of an import permit issued by the International Trade Administration (經濟部國際貿易局) before proceeding with the TFDA registration review.

### 1.4 Trademarks & Special Naming Claims
*   **Trademark Registration:** If the product name includes a registered trademark, verify the trademark registration certificate. If the name uses another manufacturer's trademark, verify a formal letter of consent from the owner.
*   **Standard Performance Terminology:** If the product name, label, packaging, or manual claims compliance with specific national or international standards, verify supporting documents from that region, a third-party validator, or the relevant international standards organization.

## 2. Product Classification & Technical Profiles

### 2.1 Class I Product Overviews (Table 1 Note 4)
*   **Requirement:** Product specifications and instructions must contain the method of use, system functions, working principles, and complete material or product composition details.
*   **Acceptance Criteria:** The documentation must provide sufficient detail to confirm the product's classification under Class I medical device definitions.

### 2.2 Class II & III Technical Profiles (Table 2 Items 2 & 8)
*   **Instruction Drafts & Labels:**
    *   *Manufacturing:* Verify drafted Chinese labels, manuals, packaging materials, and actual color photographs of the product's exterior.
    *   *Import:* Verify original foreign labels, manuals, packaging drafts, a detailed Chinese manual draft, and actual color photographs of the product's exterior.
*   **Operations & Maintenance Manuals:** For instruments and hardware systems, operation and maintenance manuals may serve as documentation for the product's structure, materials, specifications, performance, intended use, and drawings.

## 3. Imported Device Authentications (Table 2 Notes 4 & 5)

### 3.1 Certificate of Free Sale (CFS / 出產國許可製售證明正本)
*   **Content Requirements:** The CFS must state:
    1. The name and specific model/specifications of the medical device.
    2. The manufacturer's name, factory address, manufacturing status, and actual market sales status in the country of origin.
*   **Alternatives & Exemptions:**
    *   *Not regulated in country of origin:* Accepts certificates from regional health agencies or TFDA-approved institutions.
    *   *Contract Manufacturing:* Accepts certificates issued by the competent authority in either the contractor's or manufacturer's country.
    *   *Global-First Devices:* A global-first device with no similar predicate may be exempted from submitting a CFS if a TFDA on-site factory audit report and a local clinical trial report are provided.
*   **Validity & Language:**
    *   The CFS must be issued within **2 years** of the submission date and be validated by the corresponding overseas ROC embassy or representative office.
    *   Non-English documents must include a validated Chinese or English translation.

### 3.2 Power of Attorney (POA / 國外原廠授權登記書正本)
*   **Content Requirements:** The POA must state:
    1. Clear authorization from the manufacturer to the local distributor to register and maintain compliance.
    2. The authorized local distributor's name, address, product name, and model/specifications.
*   **Validity & Language:**
    *   The POA must be issued within **1 year** of the submission date.
    *   Non-English documents must include a Chinese or English translation.

## 4. Pre-clinical Testing & Lab Accreditation (Table 2 Note 7)

### 4.1 Testing Laboratory Accreditations (Note 7(5))
*   **Required Testing Fields:** Biocompatibility, electrical safety, electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), and sterility testing reports.
*   **Accreditation Threshold:** The testing laboratory must meet at least one of the following conditions:
    1. Accreditation under **ISO/IEC 17025** standards.
    2. Conformance with Good Laboratory Practice (**GLP**) guidelines.

### 4.2 Class II Pre-clinical Data Substitutions
*   **Technical Cooperation Agreement Route:** Submissions utilizing technical cooperation agreements with reciprocal countries may substitute raw testing records with a CFS and an EP/STED summary.
*   **Preclinical Conformity Statement:** Applicants who have previously registered a similar product from the same manufacturer may submit a preclinical comparison and statement of conformity (第二等級醫療器材產品比較暨臨床前測試資料符合性聲明書) in place of raw testing data.

## 5. Clinical Evidence & Exemption Pathways (Table 2 Note 9)

### 5.1 Clinical Trial & Evaluation Report Audits
*   Verify clinical evaluation reports, literature reviews, or clinical trial reports are submitted for Class II/III devices without established local predicates.
*   Confirm the testing protocols and patient cohorts match the intended indications for use.

### 5.2 Exemption Criteria for Class II Devices without Predicates
*   A Class II device without an established predicate may be exempted from submitting a clinical trial report if it meets all of the following conditions:
    1. There are no known racial differences in the device's clinical performance.
    2. The device's intended use or indications have no history of severe adverse events or product recalls in foreign markets.
    3. Differences between the subject device and established predicates do not affect safety or effectiveness, as demonstrated by preclinical testing, or the device has been approved in both the US and EU for indications that encompass the proposed local indications.

---

## 6. Bovine & Ovine Sourcing Controls (Table 2 Note 13(1))

### 6.1 BSE/TSE Prevention Checklists
*   For medical devices containing or manufactured from bovine or ovine tissues:
    1. Verify the manufacturer's animal raw material source controls and certificates of origin.
    2. Ensure that no raw materials are sourced from regions listed by the Ministry of Agriculture as active Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy (BSE) risk areas.
    3. Confirm that processing and final manufacturing steps are secure against potential BSE pathogen contamination.
